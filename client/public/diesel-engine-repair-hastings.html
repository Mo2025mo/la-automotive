Common Diesel Engine Problems

DPF (Diesel Particulate Filter) Issues

Modern diesel cars have DPF systems that can become blocked, causing:

  • DPF warning light on dashboard
  • Reduced engine power (limp mode)
  • Increased fuel consumption
  • Black smoke from exhaust

Turbocharger Problems

Diesel turbos are essential for performance but can fail, causing:

  • Loss of power and acceleration
  • Whistling or grinding noises
  • Blue or black exhaust smoke
  • Oil leaks around turbo

EGR Valve Faults

EGR valves reduce emissions but can become clogged:

  • Rough idling and stalling
  • Reduced fuel economy
  • Black smoke on acceleration
  • Engine management light

Diesel Engine Maintenance Tips

  • Regular DPF Regeneration: Take longer drives to allow DPF cleaning cycles
  • Quality Diesel Fuel: Use premium diesel to keep injectors clean
  • Oil Changes: Use correct diesel engine oil and change regularly
  • Air Filter: Replace air filter to protect turbo and engine
  • EGR Cleaning: Annual EGR valve cleaning prevents carbon buildup
  • Fuel Filter: Replace diesel fuel filter as per service schedule
